You may be able to improve your dial-up connection speed by trying one or more of the suggestions below. You can return to these tips at any time by choosing Help from the Sign-in page and choosing Improving slow dial-up connections.

Select when you have finished reviewing these tips.

Check your phone line and equipment
Listen for static or noise on the phone line. Try the following and see if the static is eliminated:

  • Make sure your phone cable is snapped securely into the back of the unit and the wall jack.
  • If you use a phone splitter, remove it and plug your directly into the phone wall jack.
  • Disconnect your answering machine, second phone, or other devices that share the phone line.
  • Try replacing your phone cord.
After doing each step, listen for static. If the above tips do not help and you still hear static on the line, contact your phone company and have them perform a frequency sweep and general line test.

Check your connection speed
With Connection History, you can look at your recent connection rates and then try to improve your connection speed.

  • From the Sign-in page, press the Favs key on your keyboard. The System Info page appears.
  • Choose Connection History. You’ll see a list that shows what happened when your player dialed ; the date and time you connected (or attempted to connect), the “Rate” (connection speed), and the access number that was dialed.
  • For even more information (such as errors that occurred), choose the blue code number to the left of a Connection History listing to view error messages and additional troubleshooting steps.

If your connection rates are consistently lower than average, you should choose more access numbers, as described below.

Choose the best access numbers

In most cases, you'll get the fastest connection speed if you choose all the local numbers on the Access Numbers page.

Note: Keep in mind that some access numbers might result in long-distance charges on your phone bill, even if the number is in your area code. Check with your phone company to make sure the numbers you’ve chosen are local calls.

If there are no additional numbers to choose from, you can scroll to the bottom of the page and choose Get more access numbers.

Tip: In general, the access numbers at the top of the list will provide the fastest connection rate.

To select access numbers:

  • Choose Settings from the Sign-in page.
  • Choose Choose connection settings.
  • Choose MSN TV access numbers, then choose Continue, The Access Numbers page appears.

The Access Numbers page gives you a few ways to try and improve your connection rate:

  • If your Connection History shows that a particular number is connecting at a slow “Rate,” you can deselect that number (remove the checkmark) on this page.
  • To increase your chances of a faster connection rate, you can select additional local numbers from the list.
  • Choose Connect.

To determine whether these tips improved your connection speed, you can check your recent connection rates in Connection History (the most recent connections are at the top of the list.)

If the tips did not improve your connection speed, contact your phone company and have them perform a frequency sweep and general line test.

Other suggestions

If you try these tips and still experience slow connection rates, you might want to consider using a broadband connection (DSL or cable modem), which will provide enhanced service. Check with your phone company or cable provider to see if they offer broadband services.

Note: Your phone company or cable provider will bill you for broadband equipment and services. You will need to pay these fees in addition to your subscription.
